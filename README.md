# Compensation

A place for contributors to the Monocle open-source project to submit compensation requests for completed work.

## Context

Monocle Exchange relies on dedicated contributors who give their time, expertise, and resources to operate an effective Haveno network. When the platform generates sufficient revenue through trade fees, contributors receive compensation.

*Note: Initially, this repository will remain inactive until the platform reaches the necessary revenue threshold to begin compensating contributors.*

## Goals

The goal of this repository is to provide an efficient, clear framework for contributors to get paid for their work. 

This repository helps us achieve the following:
* **Increase transparency** regarding project fund allocation.
* **Decentralize the management** and validation of contribution payments.
* **Pay contributors directly in Monero (XMR)** to uphold our privacy values.

## Compensation Rates

We use flat-rate structures for our defined project roles.

### User-Facing Roles
* **Community Managers:** $500 USD per month *(minimum 10 hours of active support per week required)*.
* **Support Team Members:** $500 USD per month *(minimum 10 hours of active support per week required)*.
* **Arbitrators:** $20 USD per arbitration case *(expected ticket response time within 24 hours)*.

### Network-Facing Roles
* **Monero Node Operators:** $50 USD per node, per month *(operators are expected to run multiple nodes)*.
* **Seed Node Operators:** $100 USD per node, per month.
* **Price Node Operators:** $100 USD per node, per month.
* **Markets Operator:** $50 USD per month.

### Application & Contributor-Facing Roles
* **Open-Source Application Maintainers:** $500 USD per month.
* **Open-Source Community Managers:** $500 USD per month.

## Payment Schedule & Process

We believe that while users of Monocle Exchange deserve strict transaction privacy, they also deserve complete transparency regarding how the exchange operates. All payments to contributors are published openly on this GitHub repository for public audit.

### Required Invoice Information
When requesting payment, contributors must open a GitHub issue containing:
1. **GitHub Username:** This must match the handle you use on our other platforms (SimpleX, Matrix) so peers can verify your identity and work history.
2. **Role Performed:** The specific role fulfilled (e.g., support team member, Monero node provider).
3. **USD Amount:** The flat-rate amount previously agreed upon for your role.
4. **Monero Address:** The XMR address where you wish to receive funds.

### Review & Consensus
Once a payment request is opened, team members have a window to add positive feedback, request clarifications, or react with a thumbs up/down. All active contributors are encouraged to engage with and validate the requests of peers they work alongside. 

Once the community reaches a clear consensus, the open-source community managers will process the transaction. 

### Final Verification
To maintain accountability, the community manager will finalize the request by replying with the **Transaction ID (TxID)** and the **Tx Secret Key**. This allows anyone to cryptographically confirm that the payment was successfully sent and received without exposing user identity. Contributors must accept this public transparency workflow prior to undertaking any paid tasks.

### Monthly Timeline
Payments are handled on a monthly cycle. Compensation is processed at the end of the following month for the work completed during the previous month.

| Days of the Month | Milestone Action Required |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1st – 10th** | Contributors post their role reports and contribution payment requests. |
| **11th – 20th** | Contributors review, comment on, and validate each other's work. |
| **11th – End of Month** | Open-source community managers process finalized payments. |

# Contributor Guidelines

Welcome to Monocle! Below are our core operational expectations, core values, and onboarding pipelines.

## 🔍 Proof of Work Required

All paid positions require a demonstrated history of contribution. We do not hire individuals without a visible track record. We prioritize active community members who are already voluntarily assisting others in our ecosystem.

If you are interested in a paid role, start by supporting the broader Monero ecosystem. Your proof of work does not need to be exclusive to Monocle; valid examples include:
* **Other Open-Source Projects:** Active, verifiable development or management in privacy tech.
* **The Monero Subreddit:** Regularly providing high-quality answers and engaging with users on Reddit.
* **The Haveno Matrix Room:** Actively troubleshooting and assisting peers in our primary chat rooms.

We build software that puts users first. If you proactively help the Monero community, you are exactly the type of person we want on our team.

## ⏳ First-Month Trial Period

Prospective contributors seeking ongoing paid roles must complete a **one-month uncompensated trial period**.

* **Onboarding & Support:** Our team will actively assist, train, and guide you through your new workflow.
* **Core Duties:** You will perform standard role tasks (e.g., handling support tickets or running test nodes), but these initial onboarding contributions are not eligible for financial compensation.
* **Mutual Evaluation:** This period gives you a risk-free environment to learn the workflow and ensures the role is a healthy, long-term fit for both parties.

> **Note:** Upon successful completion of the trial period and mutual agreement, you will progress to a permanent, paid role.

## 🎭 Nyms Welcome

Can a privacy-focused exchange operate transparently? We think so. Monocle is building an exchange operated entirely by pseudonymous entities (nyms) while maintaining institutional-grade public trust.

* **Strict Anonymity:** We expect all contributors to operate under pseudonyms.
* **Zero PII Collection:** We value your privacy and do not collect any personal identity information.
* **Required Data:** We only require your active nym handle and a Monero (XMR) address to issue compensation.

If your pseudonym has a proven track record in the Monero space and you share our core architectural values, reach out to us—we want to earn the community's trust alongside you.
